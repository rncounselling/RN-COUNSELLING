What is MBBS?

MBBS (Bachelor of Medicine and Bachelor of Surgery) is an undergraduate medical degree program that prepares students to become qualified medical practitioners. It is one of the most sought-after professional courses in India, requiring candidates to qualify through the National Eligibility cum Entrance Test (NEET UG). The course provides comprehensive knowledge of human anatomy, physiology, pharmacology, pathology, and clinical medicine, along with practical training in hospitals and healthcare settings.

MBBS Course Duration

Academic Study

4.5

Years of classroom learning, practical sessions, and clinical rotations covering all medical subjects

Mandatory Internship

1

Year of compulsory rotating internship in various departments to gain hands-on clinical experience

Eligibility Criteria for MBBS

NEET UG Qualification

  • Must qualify NEET UG examination
  • Score above cutoff percentile
  • Valid NEET scorecard required

Educational Qualification

  • 10+2 with Physics, Chemistry, Biology
  • English as compulsory subject
  • From recognized board

Minimum Marks Required

  • General: 50% in PCB
  • OBC/SC/ST: 40% in PCB
  • PWD: 45% in PCB

Age Criteria

  • Minimum age: 17 years
  • Maximum age: 25 years (General)
  • Age relaxation for reserved categories
