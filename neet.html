Types of NEET UG Counselling in India

1. All India Quota (AIQ) Counselling

Conducted by MCC for 15% of total seats in government medical colleges across India. Open to candidates from all states.

  • 15% seats in government medical colleges
  • 100% seats in central universities (AIIMS, JIPMER, etc.)
  • Deemed and central universities
  • Open to all Indian nationals

2. State Quota Counselling

Conducted by respective state authorities for 85% of seats in state government medical colleges. Domicile requirements apply.

  • 85% seats in state government colleges
  • State domicile required
  • Separate counselling by each state
  • Different schedules and processes

3. Deemed University Counselling

Some deemed universities conduct their own counselling process separately from MCC.

  • Institution-specific counselling
  • Higher fee structure
  • Separate application process
  • Direct admission in some cases

4. Private College Counselling

Private medical colleges participate in state counselling or conduct their own admission process.

  • Management quota seats available
  • Higher fees compared to government colleges
  • Some participate in state counselling
  • Direct admission options available

Eligibility Criteria for NEET UG 2026 Counselling

Basic Eligibility Requirements

  • NEET Qualification: Must have qualified NEET UG 2026 exam
  • Age Criteria: Minimum 17 years as of December 31, 2026
  • Upper Age Limit: No upper age limit for government colleges (as per recent updates)
  • Educational Qualification: 10+2 with Physics, Chemistry, Biology/Biotechnology and English
  • Minimum Marks: 50% for General, 40% for SC/ST/OBC, 45% for PwD

Domicile Requirements

Documents Required for Counselling

Ensure you have all the following documents ready in original and photocopy format for the counselling process:

Essential Documents

  • NEET UG 2026 Admit Card
  • NEET UG 2026 Score Card/Rank Letter
  • Class 10th Mark Sheet and Certificate
  • Class 12th Mark Sheet and Certificate
  • Date of Birth Certificate
  • Valid Photo ID Proof (Aadhar Card/PAN Card/Passport/Driving License)
  • Passport size photographs (recent)

Category-Specific Documents

  • SC/ST/OBC Candidates: Valid Caste Certificate issued by competent authority
  • EWS Candidates: EWS Certificate (issued after March 31, 2026)
  • PwD Candidates: Disability Certificate (minimum 40% disability)
  • State Quota: Domicile Certificate of respective state

Additional Documents

  • Transfer Certificate from previous institution
  • Migration Certificate (if applicable)
  • Character Certificate
  • Medical Fitness Certificate
  • Provisional Allotment Letter (after seat allotment)

Important Note

Keep multiple photocopies of all documents. Original documents will be verified during physical reporting at the allotted college. Any discrepancy in documents can lead to cancellation of admission.

Step-by-Step NEET UG 2026 Counselling Process

Step 1: Registration

  • Visit the official MCC website or respective state counselling portal
  • Register using NEET Roll Number and other details
  • Pay the registration fee (varies by category)
  • Verify email and mobile number

Step 2: Choice Filling

  • Login to your counselling account
  • Fill and lock your college and course preferences
  • You can fill up to 500+ choices in AIQ counselling
  • Arrange choices in order of preference
  • Lock your choices before the deadline

Step 3: Seat Allotment

  • Seat allotment based on rank, choices, and seat availability
  • Results announced on official website
  • Check your allotment status online
  • Download provisional allotment letter

Step 4: Acceptance and Reporting

  • Accept the allotted seat online
  • Pay seat acceptance fee
  • Report to allotted college with original documents
  • Complete document verification
  • Pay college fees and complete admission formalities

Step 5: Upgradation (If Applicable)

  • If you want to participate in next round for better college
  • Choose "Upgrade" option during seat acceptance
  • Your current seat will be retained if no upgrade is available
  • If upgraded, report to new college and withdraw from previous

Important Tips

  • Complete registration and choice filling well before deadlines
  • Keep all documents ready in advance
  • Fill maximum number of choices to increase admission chances
  • Understand the difference between "Freeze" and "Upgrade" options
  • Monitor official websites regularly for updates

Types of Seats in NEET Counselling

1. All India Quota (AIQ) Seats

15% of total seats in government medical colleges across India, allocated through MCC counselling.

  • Open to candidates from all states
  • No domicile requirement
  • Highly competitive
  • Lower fees compared to private colleges

2. State Quota Seats

85% of seats in state government medical colleges, reserved for state domicile candidates.

  • State domicile mandatory
  • Separate counselling by each state
  • Better chances for home state candidates
  • Affordable fee structure

3. Management Quota Seats

Seats in private medical colleges filled through management quota with higher fees.

  • Available in private and deemed universities
  • Higher fee structure (₹10-25 lakhs per year)
  • Direct admission possible
  • NEET qualification mandatory

4. NRI Quota Seats

Reserved seats for NRI candidates and their sponsored students.

  • Available in private and deemed universities
  • Higher fees in foreign currency
  • Separate application process
  • Limited seats available

5. Institutional Quota

Seats reserved by institutions for specific categories or internal candidates.

  • Institution-specific criteria
  • May include staff wards, alumni children
  • Separate selection process
  • Limited availability

Common Mistakes to Avoid & Tips for Success

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Missing Deadlines: Not completing registration or choice filling on time
  • Limited Choices: Filling too few college choices reduces admission chances
  • Ignoring State Counselling: Not participating in state counselling alongside AIQ
  • Wrong Choice Order: Not arranging colleges in proper preference order
  • Document Issues: Incomplete or incorrect documents leading to rejection
  • Not Understanding Options: Confusion between Freeze, Float, and Upgrade options
  • Ignoring Private Colleges: Not considering good private colleges as backup
  • Unrealistic Expectations: Only filling top colleges without realistic options
  • Missing Stray Vacancy Round: Not participating in final round for remaining seats
  • Falling for Frauds: Trusting fake counselling agents promising guaranteed admissions

Expert Tips for Successful Counselling

  • Research Thoroughly: Study previous year cutoffs and college rankings
  • Fill Maximum Choices: Use all available choice slots (500+ in AIQ)
  • Strategic Choice Filling: Mix dream, realistic, and safe colleges
  • Consider All Options: Include government, private, and deemed universities
  • Understand Seat Types: Know the difference between AIQ, state quota, and management quota
  • Keep Documents Ready: Prepare all documents well in advance
  • Monitor Regularly: Check official websites daily for updates
  • Participate in All Rounds: Don't skip any counselling round
  • Seek Expert Guidance: Consult experienced counsellors for personalized advice
  • Stay Calm: Make informed decisions without panic

Choice Filling Strategy

  • Start with your dream colleges (even if cutoff is slightly higher)
  • Fill colleges matching your expected rank range
  • Include safe options with lower cutoffs
  • Consider college location, infrastructure, and faculty
  • Don't ignore good private colleges as backup
  • Arrange choices in strict order of preference
  • Review and modify choices before final submission
