What are Allied Health Sciences?

Allied Health Sciences encompass a diverse range of healthcare professions that support and complement medical and nursing services. These programs prepare students for vital roles in diagnostic services, therapeutic interventions, and healthcare management. Popular Allied Health Science programs include B.Sc. in Medical Laboratory Technology (BMLT), Radiology, Physiotherapy, Operation Theatre Technology, Optometry, and Nursing Assistants. These courses offer excellent career opportunities in India's rapidly growing healthcare sector, providing essential support services that are crucial for patient care and medical diagnosis.

Course Duration & Structure

Most Allied Health Science programs follow a structured curriculum designed to provide both theoretical knowledge and practical skills:

  • Duration: Typically 3-4 years for undergraduate programs
  • Internship: 6-month mandatory internship in hospitals or healthcare facilities
  • Curriculum: Combination of classroom lectures, laboratory work, and clinical training
  • Specialization: Students can choose specific areas based on their interests and career goals

Eligibility Criteria

To pursue Allied Health Science courses, candidates must meet the following requirements:

  • Educational Qualification: 10+2 (Physics, Chemistry, Biology) mandatory
  • Minimum Marks: 50% aggregate for General category, 40% for Reserved categories
  • Age Requirement: Minimum 17 years of age at the time of admission
  • Entrance Exams: Some institutions conduct entrance tests, while others offer merit-based admission

Types of Allied Health Science Colleges

Allied Health Science programs are offered by various types of institutions across India:

  • Government Medical Colleges: Affiliated with state medical universities, offering quality education at affordable fees
  • Private Institutions: Well-equipped facilities with modern infrastructure and industry partnerships
  • Deemed Universities: Autonomous institutions with specialized programs and research opportunities
