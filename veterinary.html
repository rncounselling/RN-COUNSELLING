What is Veterinary Science (BVSc & AH)?

Bachelor of Veterinary Science & Animal Husbandry (BVSc & AH) is a professional degree program focused on animal healthcare, veterinary practices, and clinical studies. This comprehensive course prepares students to become veterinary doctors who can diagnose, treat, and prevent diseases in animals. The curriculum covers various aspects including animal anatomy, physiology, pathology, pharmacology, surgery, and public health. Students gain hands-on experience in treating domestic animals, livestock, and wildlife, making them competent professionals in the field of veterinary medicine.

The course emphasizes both theoretical knowledge and practical skills, ensuring graduates are well-equipped to handle the diverse challenges in animal healthcare. With growing awareness about animal welfare and the increasing demand for quality veterinary services, BVSc & AH graduates play a crucial role in maintaining animal health, improving livestock productivity, and ensuring food safety.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is NEET compulsory for BVSc admission? +

Yes, NEET-UG qualification is mandatory for admission to BVSc & AH courses in India. All veterinary colleges, whether government or private, accept admissions based on NEET scores. You must qualify NEET with the required percentile as per your category to be eligible for veterinary counselling.

What is BVSc course duration? +

The BVSc & AH course duration is 5 years of academic study followed by a mandatory 6-month rotatory internship. The total duration is 5.5 years, after which you receive your degree and become eligible to practice as a veterinary doctor. The internship provides hands-on clinical experience in various departments of veterinary hospitals.

Can I get a veterinary seat with low NEET score? +

Yes, it is possible to secure a veterinary seat even with a relatively lower NEET score, especially in private colleges and deemed universities. The cutoff varies significantly between government and private institutions. State quota seats in government colleges have lower cutoffs compared to All India quota. Expert counselling can help you identify colleges where you have realistic chances based on your NEET rank and category.

Is BVSc recognized by the Veterinary Council of India? +

Yes, all BVSc & AH programs offered by recognized veterinary colleges in India are approved and regulated by the Veterinary Council of India (VCI). The VCI ensures that colleges maintain prescribed standards of veterinary education. Always verify that your chosen college is VCI-approved before taking admission. Degrees from VCI-recognized colleges are valid for practice and further studies in India and abroad.
