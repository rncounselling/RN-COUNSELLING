What is MD / MS Counselling?

MD (Doctor of Medicine) and MS (Master of Surgery) are the two premier postgraduate medical degree programmes in India. Admission to these courses is purely merit-based through NEET PG scores, followed by a centralised counselling process conducted by the Medical Counselling Committee (MCC) for All India Quota (AIQ) seats and by respective State Counselling Authorities for state quota seats. RN Counselling provides end-to-end support to help you secure your best possible specialisation and college.

Step-by-Step MD / MS Counselling Process

1

Check Eligibility & Score

Confirm your NEET PG score, rank, and category. Verify that your internship is completed and your MCI/NMC/State Medical Council registration is active.

2

Register on MCC Portal

Visit mcc.nic.in during the registration window. Enter your NEET PG Roll Number, date of birth, and personal details. Pay the non-refundable registration fee (Government seat: ₹1,000 approx; Private seat: ₹5,000 approx).

3

Document Verification

Upload scanned copies of all required documents. Ensure all documents are valid, legible, and correctly formatted before submission.

4

Choice Filling & Locking

Search and shortlist MD/MS specialisations and colleges. Arrange them in your preferred order. This is the most critical step — strategy matters. Lock your choices before the deadline.

5

Seat Allotment Result

MCC publishes the seat allotment result online. Log in to check your allotted college and course. Carefully review the allotment before deciding to accept or upgrade.

6

Accept Seat & Pay Security Deposit

If satisfied, accept the allotted seat online and pay the security deposit to hold it. Failure to accept within the deadline results in cancellation of allotment.

7

Report to Allotted College

Visit the allotted college within the reporting window with all original documents. Complete admission formalities, fee payment, and submit required documents at the college.

8

Participate in Further Rounds (Optional)

If you wish to upgrade to a better college or specialisation, participate in Round 2 or Mop-Up. Your previous allotment is cancelled upon accepting a new seat.
